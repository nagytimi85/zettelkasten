When I took [[hub-zettelkasten|Zettelkasten]] coaching with [[ref-fast-sascha|Sascha Fast]], he recommended the Knowledge Flower technique to deepen our thinking about a topic and the Idea Compass technique to link it to other notes in a meaningful way. 

While they are great techniques, I frankly found them to be way above my level. 

For beginners (such as myself :)), I'd rather suggest Nick Milo's very simple and straight-forward techniques, the "this is interesting, _because_..." prompt for thinking about a topic (and not just collecting it) and the "this reminds of..." prompt for linking. 


## In summary and in comparison:

For deepening our thinking:
- "this is interesting, _because_..."
- Knowledge Flower

For enriching our net of linked notes:
- "this reminds me of..."
- Idea Compass

|                   | For deepening our thinking          | For enriching our net of linked notes |
| ----------------- | ----------------------------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| for beginners     | "this is interesting, _because_..." | "this reminds me of..."               |
| for intermediates | Knowledge Flower                    | Idea Compass                          |
