"Behind every successful man, there is a woman", goes the saying. 

But for me, it's actually triggering to look at the schedule of successful men, often listed as an inspiration or template for a successful day. 

You see things like:
- I wake up, wash myself, get dressed, eat breakfast and start to work
- At noon, I eat lunch and go for a walk with my dog
- I get back to work in the afternoon
- I eat dinner, then I spend time with my correspondence or I read.
- I go to bed.

No mention of how clean clothes, breakfast, lunch or dinner appears, or how the house stays clean, the pantry remains stocked. 

The work of women (or servants - but even the most humble man can afford at least a wife as a servant) is the grout that keeps these successful schedules together.
