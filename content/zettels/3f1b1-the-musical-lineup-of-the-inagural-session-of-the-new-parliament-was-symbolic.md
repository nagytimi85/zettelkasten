The musical lineup of the inaugural session of the new Parliament of Hungary in 2026 was symbolic. 

The standard parts of such lineup are:
- Himnusz - the national anthem ("O God, bless the nation of Hungary")
- Szózat - our "second national anthem" ("To your homeland without fail / Be faithful, O Hungarian!)

On top of these, the following songs were presented in the chamber as the session closed:
- Cigányhimhusz (Zöld az erdő, zöld a hegy is) - the Romani anthem ("Green is the forest, green is the mountain") - as a gesture towards the Romani population of Hungary
- Tavaszi szél vizet áraszt - the folk song that became the unofficial anthem of the winning TISZA party ("Spring wind floods water")
- Ode to Joy - the anthem of the European Union - played as a symbol of Hungary re-committing to Europe
- Székely himnusz - the anthem of Székely Land / Transylvania ("Who knoweth where, O where our destiny shall be?") - played as a gesture towards Hungarians abroad, also as a gesture towards the nationalists

On the steps of the Parliament, there were additional performances, some of them also had heavy significance or symbolism:
- Magyarország, a wholesome patriotic song performed by Ibolya Oláh had its multi-layered symbolism and significance
	- [[3f1b1a-ibolya-olah-performing-magyarorszag-on-inauguration-day-had-a-multi-layered-symbolism-and-significane]]
- Georgina Revell (Jalja) performed Midnight (Hanging tree - a Hunger Games music remix) live and Dr. Zsolt Hegedűs, incoming health minister performed his viral dance to it. 
	- He went viral with his spontaneous joyful dance moves to this song, celebrating on Election night.
	- See also: [[3b1a3a-we-learned-from-the-fidesz-election-campaign-meeting-hegedus-dance-moves-that-you-can-t-be-performatively-youth-friendly]]
	- Martin from the Hiperventi Youtube Channel pointed out that Hegedűs' performance, along with the light show, Péter Magyar DJ-ing during the night, and the rave party until morning were not just the signs of change but a serious nod to the core base that elected TISZA: the youth.
