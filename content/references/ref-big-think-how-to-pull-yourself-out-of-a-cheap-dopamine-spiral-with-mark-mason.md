Mark Manson is the writer of The ​Subtle Art of Not Giving a F.ck


Happiness =/ hedonism

Hedonism = seeking highs

Happiness = when you look back, you feel like your life was worth living

Adolescence = bargaining for social status and approval, seeing the world as transactional

Adulthood (happy adulthood) =

- “not giving a [f.ck](http://f.ck)” about social status and approval
- focusing on the things that are “worth giving a [f.ck](http://f.ck)” (that will make your life worth living upon looking back, that is worth not being liked for)
- being aware of the pains you are secretly happy to endure (either because it’s your weirdness, or because they come with the things that are worth giving a [f.ck](http://f.ck))
- stop seeing the world as transactional
- maintaining your virtues regardless of what you get back

Adulthood is anti-fragile

Related:

- my weirdness (pains I’m happy to endure) helps me to be a good administrator
- compare Aristotle’s 12 virtues with Franklin’s 13 virtues
- for leaving adolescence behind, see also that C.S. Lewis quote about childish things, which in an embellished way also says: stop giving a [f.ck](http://f.ck) about what others think
