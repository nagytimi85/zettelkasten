## Notes

One of the main characters explain the title and essence of the story this way:

There are two ways of gaining back someone’s glory (pride, dignity that was taken away):

- either by practicing forgiveness,
- or by carrying out revenge

One antagonist is in close connection to a Christian congregation. Through that plot line, there is some exploration around the thought that practicing forgiveness (the Christian way) is only a more valid way if the Christian God exists. Given that the context is South Korean, unlike in a European setting, the Christian way is easier to question, contrast, mark as optional.

Vengefulness and forgiveness is also an Old Testament vs New Testament juxtaposition.
